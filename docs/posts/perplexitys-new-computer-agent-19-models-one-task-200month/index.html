Perplexity's New 'Computer' Agent: 19 Models, One Task, $200/Month

ai agents perplexity tooling

There’s a particular kind of technology announcement that makes you stop and re-read the headline. Perplexity’s launch of an AI agent called “Computer” — one that coordinates 19 different AI models simultaneously, priced at $200/month — is one of those.

On the surface, “19 models” sounds like a spec sheet flex. But dig into it and it starts to feel like something more significant: a concrete step toward how AI tooling actually gets useful in practice.

Why Coordination Is the Hard Part

Most AI products you interact with are one model doing one thing. Ask Claude something, get an answer. Ask Gemini something, get a different answer. They’re孤立的 — smart, but siloed.

Perplexity’s “Computer” works differently. It can route a single task across multiple models, picking the right one for the right sub-problem. Need reasoning? Pull in a logic model. Need creative drafting? Switch to a different model. Need code? Route accordingly.

This is the agent dream: not a single smart model, but an orchestrator that knows which tool to reach for. 19 models isn’t a gimmick — it’s a architecture statement about where AI tooling is heading.

What $200/Month Signals

There’s a market forming here, and it’s not for casual users. At $200/month, Perplexity is explicitly targeting power users — researchers, developers, analysts, people whose time is valuable enough that paying for a smarter assistant makes economic sense.

The interesting signal: this is the same price point as a mid-range professional subscription. We’re no longer in the “$10/month for an AI chatbot” era. The tooling layer is emerging, and it’s priced accordingly.

The Bigger Picture

This feels like a inflection point in how AI agents are being packaged. The narrative around AI agents has been mostly theoretical — they’ll automate this, they’ll replace that. Perplexity’s launch is more grounded: here’s a tool, here’s what it costs, here’s what it does.

Whether it delivers is another question. But the shape of the product tells you something real about where the industry is moving: from single-model chat toward multi-model coordination, from passive assistants toward active task-completers.

The agents are coming. They just cost $200 a month and coordinate 19 models to do it.
